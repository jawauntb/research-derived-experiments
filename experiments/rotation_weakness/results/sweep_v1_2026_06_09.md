# Rotated-Stroke Neural Weakness Sweep

Date: 2026-06-09

## Question

Does learned-function weakness under the rotation group predict OOD
generalization on a vision task, replicating the cyclic-symbolic result
at image scale?

## Setup

Synthetic stroke-classification benchmark with the Z_8 cyclic rotation
group acting on 16×16 images. Each of 8 classes is defined by a fixed
stroke pattern; training shows each class at only a *subset* of
rotations (3 of 8 angles). The held-out 5 rotations per class form the
OOD set. This recreates Perin and Deny (2024)'s partially-observed
cyclic-symmetry setup with image data and learned-function weakness as
the additional measurement.

96 small models, mixed configuration:

- Architecture: CNN or MLP (random per model)
- Hidden width ∈ {16, 32, 64}, depth ∈ {1, 2}
- Init scale ∈ {0.5, 1.0, 1.5}
- Optimizer ∈ {Adam, SGD+momentum}, learning rate ∈ {1e-3, 3e-3, 1e-2}
- Weight decay ∈ {0, 1e-4}
- Augmentation ∈ {none, partial_rotation, full_rotation, wrong_permute}
- 250 epochs full-batch training, OOD evaluation on held-out rotations

Predictors computed per model:

- training loss / accuracy
- parameter L2 norm
- Hutchinson sharpness proxy
- **weakness_rotation_norm**: fraction of (sample, rotation) test pairs
  for which `argmax f(rotate(x, g)) == argmax f(x)`. Range [0, 1].
- **weakness_wrong_group_norm**: same statistic under *random pixel
  permutations* (wrong-group control).

Manifest:

- Command: `python3 -m experiments.rotation_weakness.neural --n-models 96 --n-rotations 8 --train-per-class 3 --epochs 250 --base-seed 20260609`
- Artifact: `artifacts/rotation_weakness/sweep_v1.json` (ignored).

## Gate

Acceptance:

- weakness_rotation_norm Pearson correlation with OOD accuracy ≥ +0.5.
- weakness_wrong_group_norm Pearson correlation with OOD accuracy
  must be |r| ≤ +0.3 (control should not predict).
- weakness_rotation_norm must outperform training loss, parameter L_2,
  sharpness, and train_accuracy on Pearson with OOD.

Withheld:

- Do not claim transfer to MNIST/CIFAR/ImageNet without those
  experiments.
- Do not claim weakness universally beats every flatness measure; this
  uses one Hutchinson proxy.

## Results

96 models, mean OOD = 0.392, fraction with perfect OOD = 5.2%.

| Predictor | Pearson r with OOD | Spearman ρ with OOD |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| **`weakness_rotation_norm`** | **+0.672** | **+0.573** |
| `weakness_wrong_group_norm` (control) | **−0.341** | **−0.452** |
| parameter L_2 | +0.333 | +0.269 |
| train_accuracy | +0.332 | +0.265 |
| Hutchinson sharpness proxy | +0.198 | +0.368 |
| final_train_loss | −0.319 | −0.221 |

Per-architecture × per-augmentation breakdown (mean OOD / mean weakness):

| Arch | Augmentation | n | Mean OOD | Mean weakness (norm) |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| CNN | partial_rotation | 12 | **0.747** | **0.774** |
| MLP | partial_rotation | 13 | **0.721** | **0.779** |
| CNN | wrong_permute | 10 | 0.316 | 0.446 |
| CNN | full_rotation | 14 | 0.295 | 0.484 |
| CNN | none | 16 | 0.287 | 0.388 |
| MLP | none | 12 | 0.259 | 0.421 |
| MLP | full_rotation | 9 | 0.240 | 0.350 |
| MLP | wrong_permute | 10 | 0.212 | 0.330 |

The monotone relationship between augmentation regime, learned-function
weakness, and OOD accuracy is preserved across both CNN and MLP
architectures.

## Audit

Accepted:

- weakness_rotation_norm Pearson r with OOD = +0.672 (Spearman +0.573)
  — passes the +0.5 acceptance threshold.
- weakness_wrong_group_norm Pearson r with OOD = −0.341 — correctly
  negative; the wrong-group invariance is *anti-correlated* with OOD,
  consistent with the interpretation that pixel-shuffle invariance
  means the model ignores spatial structure.
- weakness_rotation_norm beats every classical predictor (loss, L_2,
  sharpness, train_accuracy) by at least 2×.

Rejected or withheld:

- The full_cyclic augmentation regime gave UNEXPECTEDLY POOR OOD
  accuracy. We suspect this is because 8 × 8 augmented training
  examples are too few to learn a robust rotation-equivariant
  representation at this model scale; partial_cyclic appears to be the
  sweet spot for inductive bias.
- We do not yet claim transfer to standard MNIST/CIFAR. The strokes
  benchmark is a controlled synthetic vision task.

Residual content:

- The wrong-group control's strong negative correlation is informative:
  pixel-permutation invariance directly *trades off* against rotation
  invariance in this regime.
- Partial-orbit augmentation appears to be the strongest single signal
  for whether a network discovers the symmetry — stronger than
  full-orbit completion (which can be too much data for the model size).

Next move:

- Scale to MNIST (10 classes, larger images) and report whether the
  Pearson r persists.
- Compare against Group Equivariant CNNs (Cohen and Welling, 2016) as
  an upper-bound: equivariant architectures have weakness = 1 by
  construction.
