# Suite C Re-Engagement Under World Change - Pre-Registration

Frozen: 2026-07-06, before the Suite C Modal L4 dispatch.

## Question

Can a finite agent re-open inquiry after a world change, recover attribution,
quiet down without false calm, and re-open inquiry again after a second shift?

The benchmark is the terminal Suite C gate for the causally grounded finite
agents program. It consolidates prior evidence:

- Paper 22: learned current-replay probing becomes quiet and fails to re-engage
  after the world changes.
- Paper 23A: surprise restores post-shift probing but over-fires.
- Paper 23B: decision-layer cooling is safer than suppressing surprise, because
  direct signal suppression can create false calm.

## Discovery-Regime Audit

Current regime:
- Artifact types: paper-local reports, probe-density summaries, and
  self/world attribution papers.
- Operations: world-change rollout, probe decision policies, cost controls,
  matched-random inquiry, result figures, benchmark cards, and critical reviews.
- Gates/verifiers: post-shift affected probe density, final component MAE,
  no-false-calm, cost discipline, and second-shift reopenability.
- Known limitations: controlled finite simulator, not a biological, neural
  validity, consciousness, or production reliability claim.

Action class:
- Discovery, narrowly: the run creates a suite-level benchmark artifact and
  acceptance gate that the older paper-local regime did not have.

## Conditions

Decision-layer candidates:

- `decision_refractory`
- `burst_then_refractory`
- `learned_cooldown_head`

Required controls:

- `p22_learned_current_replay`: learned-silence baseline.
- `two_timescale_plus_prediction_error`: anxious surprise baseline.
- `fixed_surprise_decrement`: signal-layer false-calm negative control.
- `scheduled_null_anchor`: high-cost positive control.
- `oracle_source`: semantic high-cost reference.
- `matched_random_time_budget`: random inquiry at the decision-refractory probe
  budget.

## Suite C Gates

| Gate | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| C1. Silence replication | P22 baseline has near-zero affected-bucket post-shift probes. |
| C2. Re-engagement | Candidate affected post-shift probe density is at least 0.5x pre-shift and at least 2x unaffected buckets. |
| C3. Recovery | Candidate final affected-component MAE is at or below the suite threshold in most seeds. |
| C4. No false calm | Probe quieting is paired with attribution-error reduction; direct signal suppression fails. |
| C5. Cost-aware inquiry | Candidate uses fewer probes than scheduled/oracle controls at comparable recovery and beats matched-random selectivity. |
| C6. Re-openability | Candidate affected probes rise again after a second shift relative to the pre-second-shift window. |

## Acceptance Rule

Accept Suite C as terminally benchmarked if all C1-C6 gates pass for at least one
decision-layer candidate and the required controls behave as controls.

## Rejection / Withholding Rule

Do not claim Suite C closure if:

- re-engagement happens but final attribution recovery fails;
- recovery requires scheduled/oracle-level probe cost;
- quiet is produced by direct surprise suppression;
- matched-random budget performs comparably to the candidate;
- second-shift re-openability fails.

## Allowed Claim

If the gate passes, the allowed claim is:

> In a controlled finite benchmark, decision-layer cooling can close the
> re-engagement bottleneck by preserving surprise as information while regulating
> the probe action tendency.

The result does not certify consciousness, broad autonomy, biological
habituation, or production reliability.
