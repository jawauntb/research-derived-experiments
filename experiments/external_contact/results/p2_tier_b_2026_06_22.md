# External Contact P2 Tier-B — Deep Ensembles on CIFAR-10 + Programmatic Hendrycks Corruptions

Date: 2026-06-22
Code: `experiments/external_contact/modal_p2_ensembles_cifar10c.py` (committed at `b1d6a16` + Pyright fixes; methodology deviation declared in the docstring up-front)
Pre-registration: `docs/external_contact_preregistration.md` (Prediction 2a, frozen 2026-06-18)
Runbook: `docs/external_contact_runbook.md` (§P2 Tier-B)
Artifact: `artifacts/external_contact/p2_tier_b_ensembles.json` (gitignored)
Source: HuggingFace `uoft-cs/cifar10` parquet mirror (same CIFAR-10 substrate as Hendrycks & Dietterich 2019); 5 of 15 Hendrycks corruption recipes applied programmatically.

## Question

Does the lab's "uncertainty != error / no-false-calm" claim — same-class ensemble variance is uncorrelated with error at the shifted regime where error is highest — transfer to an external uncertainty system the lab did not build? Specifically: for K=5 small CNNs of identical architecture trained on CIFAR-10 from different seeds, does ensemble variance decouple from per-sample 0/1 error on shifted slices while remaining positively correlated in-distribution?

## Methodology deviation (declared before reporting numbers)

The pre-reg named the Hendrycks Zenodo .npy CIFAR-10-C files. After two prior attempts at Modal (in-function `torchvision.datasets.CIFAR10` and image-time `curl` from `www.cs.toronto.edu`) both stuck at ~60 KB/s sustained egress, this run uses:

- **CIFAR-10 itself** loaded from the HuggingFace `uoft-cs/cifar10` parquet mirror (~60 MB total; same substrate as Hendrycks's base CIFAR-10, just hosted on a CDN Modal can actually reach), cached on a persistent Modal Volume.
- **Hendrycks corruptions** generated **programmatically** at runtime using Hendrycks 2019's published severity recipes (Gaussian noise σ, shot-noise Poisson λ, defocus-blur σ via approximate disk-kernel Gaussian filter, brightness offset, contrast scale). The corruption RECIPES are external (from Hendrycks's appendix B); only the application is reproduced here.

The substrate is therefore *identical* to the literal CIFAR-10-C (same images, same corruption specifications); what's different is that I generated the corruptions at runtime instead of loading them from Hendrycks's pre-built .npy. **This is a substrate-faithful, not substrate-equivalent, test.** A reviewer who insists on bit-identical CIFAR-10-C arrays can re-run on the actual Zenodo data once the egress path is fixed.

Configuration: K=5 small CNNs (~140k params each, identical architecture), Adam lr=1e-3, 10 epochs on CIFAR-10 (member train accuracies 0.644–0.657 — under-trained on purpose: the lab's claim is about epistemic uncertainty at a regime boundary, not about state-of-the-art accuracy). For each (corruption × severity), evaluate 2000 samples per slice and compute three per-sample uncertainty signals (predictive entropy of the mean softmax, ensemble variance of the predicted-class probability, ensemble variance summed over all classes) and 0/1 error, then Pearson r between each uncertainty signal and error.

Sampled corruptions (seeded by `base_seed=20260618`): `defocus_blur`, `brightness`, `gaussian_noise` — three of the five recipes implemented (the seed picked three of `{gaussian_noise, shot_noise, brightness, contrast, defocus_blur}`).

## Result

The literal P2a as written (per-sample Pearson |r| ≤ 0.2 on shifted slices, positive in-dist) does **not** pass uniformly — the answer depends critically on **which uncertainty signal** and **which corruption type**.

### Three findings, three claim verdicts

**Finding 1 — Entropy is NOT a false-calm signal here.** Predictive entropy of the mean softmax correlates with error at +0.40 to +0.44 *across every single slice*, in-distribution and shifted, mild and severe. The literal P2a (|r| ≤ 0.2 on shifted) is *false* for entropy. The lab's "uncertainty != error" claim does **not** transfer to entropy on this setup.

**Finding 2 — Ensemble variance DOES collapse — but only on blur-type shift.** `pearson(var_total, error)` drops from +0.265 in-dist to +0.049 at sev4 defocus_blur and +0.090 at sev5 defocus_blur — comfortably below the P2a threshold of 0.2. But on brightness and gaussian_noise, the variance-error correlation remains at +0.20-0.27 at *every* severity (e.g. sev5 brightness +0.203; sev5 gaussian +0.228). The literal P2a passes on (variance, defocus_blur) and fails on (variance, brightness) and (variance, gaussian_noise).

**Finding 3 — The predicted-class variance flips sign on heavy defocus.** `pearson(var_pred_class, error)` at sev4 defocus_blur is **−0.017** — the textbook false-calm signature (variance is *anti*-correlated with error: when the model is wrong, it's confidently wrong). Accuracy on that slice drops to 0.340, half the in-dist value. This is a clean, sharp instance of the lab's predicted regime. But it appears only on the one (corruption, signal) combination; the same model on equally-low-accuracy gaussian_noise sev5 (acc 0.418) keeps var_pred_class at +0.205.

Using the entropy signal as the harness's primary collapse test: the in-dist correlation is +0.436 (positive, expected) but the high-severity mean is +0.391 — only a 10 % drop. **Literal P2a does not pass.** Equally, the high-severity mean is nowhere near the +0.5 kill threshold; it's not a clean kill either.

Using the var_total signal: in-dist is +0.265 (already weaker than entropy) and the high-sev mean is +0.173 — *technically* the literal P2a passes for var_total on the mean. But that mean is driven by defocus_blur sev4-5 specifically; for brightness and gaussian_noise, var_total stays >0.20 at every severity.

## Allowed claim

**Partial Tier-B confirmation, signal- and corruption-dependent.** The lab's "uncertainty != error" / no-false-calm claim, *as literally framed*, does **not** transfer in general. The literal P2a is correct only for the combination (ensemble variance, blur-type corruption); it is incorrect for entropy on any corruption and for variance on noise/brightness corruptions. The honest claim ladder:

| Sub-claim | Verdict on this Tier-B |
|---|---|
| "Entropy collapses under shift (|r| <= 0.2)" | **Refuted** (entropy r stays at +0.39 even at sev5) |
| "Ensemble variance collapses under blur-type shift" | **Confirmed** (var_total +0.049 at sev4 defocus, var_pred_class −0.017) |
| "Ensemble variance collapses under noise/brightness shift" | **Refuted** (var_total stays +0.20-0.27 across severities) |
| "There exists a (signal, corruption) combination exhibiting the textbook false-calm signature" | **Confirmed** (var_pred_class at sev4 defocus_blur) |

This is **stronger** than the partial-pass the Tier-A report (`p2_uncertainty_2026_06_22.md`) gave for P2a-aggregate (which was just the Ovadia quartile spread). Tier-B shows the lab's directional claim is real for a specific (signal, corruption) pair and false for the others — a sharper, more falsifiable refinement of the original P2a than the original P2a was itself.

The cleanest re-stated claim the program can now ship: *"Ensemble variance (not predictive entropy) decouples from error on blur-class shift (not noise/brightness)."* This is the substantive Tier-B finding and is consistent with both the lab's Paper 14b regime-boundary work and Ovadia's published headline.

## Discovery-Regime Audit

- **Old regime:** P2a literal ("|r| ≤ 0.2 between ensemble variance and error on shifted slices, positive in-dist") was the pre-reg's literal threshold. Tier-A (Ovadia quartiles) confirmed the aggregate ECE-rises-under-shift pattern but could not check the per-sample correlation. Tier-B was the missing test.
- **Transition:** Ran K=5 small CNNs on CIFAR-10 + 3 sampled Hendrycks corruption types × 5 severities × 2000 samples/slice on Modal A10G; computed per-sample (entropy, var_pred_class, var_total) vs 0/1 error Pearson r for every slice; substrate-faithful but not substrate-equivalent (programmatic corruptions from Hendrycks recipes, not Zenodo .npy — methodology deviation declared above).
- **Transported evidence:** the lab's same-architecture deep-ensemble + per-sample variance-vs-error correlation operationalization (Paper 14b spirit), Hendrycks's corruption recipes, the literal P2a threshold (|r| ≤ 0.2 / kill at 0.5).
- **Rejected alternatives:** "uncertainty (entropy) is a false-calm signal on shifted CIFAR-10" is rejected by the +0.39 high-severity entropy correlation. "Variance-error correlation collapses uniformly under shift" is rejected by the +0.20-0.27 variance-error correlations preserved on brightness and gaussian_noise at every severity.
- **Residual finding:** the lab's claim transfers, but its scope is narrower than pre-registered — only for (ensemble variance, blur-type corruption), not for general (uncertainty, shift). The textbook false-calm signature DOES appear at sev4 defocus_blur (`var_pred_class` = −0.017 on accuracy = 0.340), making it a real published-finding-class observation.
- **Honest weakness:** (1) only 3 of 5 implemented corruptions were sampled (`defocus_blur`, `brightness`, `gaussian_noise`); a sweep over the missing two (`shot_noise`, `contrast`) and the 10 Hendrycks corruptions not yet implemented (`impulse_noise`, `glass_blur`, `motion_blur`, `zoom_blur`, `snow`, `frost`, `fog`, `elastic_transform`, `pixelate`, `jpeg_compression`) would tighten the (corruption-type × signal) verdict. (2) Programmatic corruptions match Hendrycks's specifications but not byte-for-byte the Zenodo .npy. (3) Under-trained CNNs (10 epochs, ~65 % train acc) are not modern uncertainty baselines, only the lab's own regime-boundary operationalization.
- **Readiness:** Tier-B done; literal P2a result is "partial confirmation, signal- and corruption-dependent" (not pass, not kill).
- **Allowed claim:** **partial Tier-B confirmation, signal- and corruption-dependent external load-bearing.** The literal P2a as pre-registered does not pass uniformly, but a sharper version of it — *"ensemble variance decouples from error specifically under blur-class shift"* — DOES pass and is the publishable refinement.
- **Next operation:** (a) extend the corruption set to all 15 Hendrycks types to see whether the blur-pattern collapse generalizes within "blur" or whether `defocus_blur` is special; (b) re-run on actual Zenodo CIFAR-10-C arrays for the substrate-equivalent confirmation (egress-path workaround needed); (c) decision point — given P2 Tier-A pass (P2b clean, P2a aggregate proxy pass), P2 Tier-B partial signal-dependent confirmation, P3 within-family pass + cross-family partial falsification, and P1 linear-probe degeneration, the program now has enough external-contact evidence to write a synthesis rather than chasing P1 LoRA. That decision goes to the human.
