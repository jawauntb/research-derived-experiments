# Discovery EWS — Retrospective Scan of the Program's Own History

Date: 2026-06-18
Code: `experiments/discovery_ews/discovery_ews.py`
Artifact: `artifacts/discovery_ews/scan.json`

## What this is (and is not)

A retrospective proxy for "how productively do this program's destabilizations
resolve?" — built because the insight literature's surprisal signal
(Tabatabaeian et al., PNAS 2025) is *structurally non-specific*: rising surprisal
is identical for a breakthrough, a stable delusion, and losing the plot. The
discriminator (transfer-survival + external load-bearing) is necessarily extrinsic
and lagging. So this tool does **not** estimate "are we about to break through"
(the flattering, motivated-reasoning version). It scores the program's *past*
spike → load-bearing **conversion rate**.

It treats result reports + paper artifacts as a symbolic time series of experiment
families (ordered by filename dates; git commit dates are useless — the repo was
seeded in bulk), computes a program-level surprisal trajectory, finds
destabilization episodes (a dormant/new family activating), and classifies how each
resolved via a transparent rubric (`load_bearing` / `self_sealing` / `dissipated`).

## The headline (robust to the rubric's known errors)

- **Spike → load-bearing conversion rate ≈ 0.12** (6 load-bearing of 51 episodes).
- **Episode verdicts: 37 self_sealing, 8 dissipated, 6 load_bearing.**
- **Generativity ratio ≈ 0.83 artifacts per family (41 families, 34 papers) — ~1:1.**

Read together: the overwhelming majority of the program's destabilizations
**self-seal** — they pass their own gates but show no held-out transfer and no
contact with a system the lab did not build. Almost nothing is externally
load-bearing. The generativity ratio is ~1:1 ("excessive agglomeration: 12 elements
→ 12 spaces"), not the "informed simplicity" downturn (few weak principles → many
phenomena) that would mark a breakthrough. **This quantifies, from the program's
own record, the "rigorous play in a self-built world" critique.** The qualitative
picture is stable even though individual verdicts are noisy (below).

## The detector hallucinated on v1 — and that is the point

The first run labeled **`activation_geometry` (the known dead-end graveyard)
"load_bearing"** and **`boundary_priors` (passed all four gates) "dissipated."**
Causes were pure apophenia:

- bare "pythia"/"gpt-2" mentions (the *probed substrate*, not external contact) fired
  the external signal 480×;
- an internal "label-free **transfer**" method term fired the transfer signal;
- "0.000" (a legitimate metric value) fired the failure signal.

Fixing it required **extrinsic ground truth** — my prior knowledge that
activation_geometry is a dead end. That is the same lesson the whole exercise is
about: a surprise/pattern signal cannot certify its own meaning; it needs contact
with something outside itself. The detector models the problem it measures.

## Known remaining misclassifications (audit, do not trust blindly)

- `symbolic_weakness` → **falsely "dissipated."** It is the flagship that became
  `weakness_invariance_neurips`; it reports success as Wilson-CI correlations, not
  "gates," so the rubric misses it. A genuine false negative.
- `concept_geometry` → likely too harsh ("dissipated").
- `activation_geometry` → too generous ("self_sealing"); it is closer to dissipated,
  but its many `heldout_*` control filenames trip the transfer signal regardless of
  pass/fail.

These are bidirectional, so they do not rescue the headline: the program is
overwhelmingly self-sealing either way.

## How to read it going forward

This is a **lagging mirror, not a warmth meter.** A rising surprisal trajectory
(e.g., the 06-18 bisociation across boundary_priors / Levin / the PNAS insight paper
/ external contact) is *not* evidence of an impending breakthrough — by
construction. The only thing that would move the conversion rate is a past
destabilization later turning out to **survive transfer and bear external load**.
Today's external-contact pre-registrations (P1–P3) are the first episodes designed
to be scoreable as `load_bearing`; none has resolved yet (P3 is blocked on GloVe
egress; see `docs/external_contact_runbook.md`).

## Next (principled version)

Replace regex-over-markdown with **structured provenance**: have each experiment
emit its gate verdicts and tier as JSON (boundary_priors and concerned_syntax
already do), and score resolution from those structured records instead of text.
That removes the apophenia and ties the tool to the discovery-regime-audit ledger.
The headline conversion metric and the generativity-ratio trend are the two numbers
worth tracking each session.
