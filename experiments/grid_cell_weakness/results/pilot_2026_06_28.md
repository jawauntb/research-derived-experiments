# Grid-Cell Weakness — Local Design Pilot (2026-06-28)

Pre-registration: [papers/grid_cell_weakness/preregistration.md](../../../papers/grid_cell_weakness/preregistration.md).
Harness: `experiments/grid_cell_weakness/{core,pilot}.py`. Run: CPU, local.
Homology backend: `gudhi` 3.12 (ripser's transitive build is broken on this image;
`gudhi` ships manylinux wheels). This pilot validates the harness only — it is **not**
gate evidence; the pre-registered gates are evaluated by the Modal sweep.

## 1. Metric discrimination (the decisive pilot check)

Can the topology + weakness metrics actually separate a toroidal code from a plane or
sphere? On synthetic manifolds with known topology (n=256, noise 0.02):

| Manifold | weakness (wrapped transl.) | β₁ estimate | H₁ top-2 lifetimes | H₂ top | toroidal_score | torus match |
|---|---:|---:|---|---:|---:|:--:|
| **torus** | **0.998** | **2** | [1.38, 1.36] | 1.24 | **0.823** | ✅ |
| plane | 0.300 | 22 | [0.06, 0.05] | 0.00 | 0.001 | ❌ |
| sphere | 0.700 | 15 | [0.24, 0.23] | 0.00 | 0.000 | ❌ |

**Result: DISCRIMINATES = True.** The harness recovers exactly the torus signature
(β₁=2 with two equal-length loops, a persistent H₂ void) and nothing toroidal for the
plane or sphere. Weakness under *wrapped* translation is also highest for the torus
(0.998) — confirming the wrap is what makes weakness separate a periodic code from a
merely translation-equivariant plane (0.300). This is the go/no-go check for the whole
experiment, and it passes cleanly.

## 2. End-to-end RNN smoke (pipeline validation)

Six tiny path-integration RNNs (Ng=Np=64, T=18, 200 steps, CPU) across augmentation
conditions; full per-net pipeline train → weakness → topology → fourier → OOD:

| condition | seed | loss | OOD acc | weakness | toroidal_score | fourier_pr |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| full_translation | …628 | 1.746 | 0.131 | 0.074 | 0.030 | 21.5 |
| full_translation | …629 | 1.738 | 0.165 | 0.134 | 0.011 | 13.9 |
| none | …628 | 1.680 | 0.235 | 0.244 | 0.007 | 18.8 |
| none | …629 | 1.375 | 0.365 | 0.418 | 0.030 | 8.7 |
| wrong_group | …628 | 1.633 | 0.228 | 0.117 | 0.027 | 14.6 |
| wrong_group | …629 | 1.508 | 0.248 | 0.156 | 0.156→0.008 | 15.9 |

Directional correlations (n=6, **sanity only, not gate evidence**):
`ρ(weakness, OOD) = +0.89`, `ρ(weakness, −fourier_pr) = +0.49`,
`ρ(weakness, topology) = −0.43`.

**Reading.** The pipeline runs end to end and produces finite, sane numbers. As expected
at pilot scale, **no torus emerges** — 200 CPU steps do not drive grid-cell formation, so
every net's `toroidal_score ≈ 0` and the weakness↔topology correlation is noise. The
encouraging `ρ(weakness, OOD)=+0.89` is consistent with the flagship but underpowered at
n=6. The real test (grid-cell emergence, β₁=2 nets, the six gates) requires the Modal
sweep: larger nets, ~4000 steps, activity regularization.

## What this pilot does not claim

It does not pass any pre-registered gate, does not show grid-cell emergence, and makes no
claim about biological data. It establishes only that the measurements separate the
topology classes they must separate, and that the training/measurement pipeline is sound.
