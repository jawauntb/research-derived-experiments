# Symbolic Weakness Multi-Family Benchmark

Date: 2026-06-09

## Question

Does symmetry-compatible-hypothesis weakness predict out-of-distribution
generalization across multiple finite-group symbolic task families, and
does it survive ablations on the candidate transformation group?

## Setup

Four task families with biased training support that admit at least one
local shortcut, at least one wrong invariant, and the true invariant:

- `cyclic_prefix_shift`  (Z_n, n in {7, 11, 13})
- `dihedral_reflection`  (D_n, n in {7, 9, 11, 13})
- `parity_coset`         (Z_2, n in {6, 8, 10})
- `color_permutation`    (S_n, n in {4, 5, 6})

Eleven selectors are scored: `train_loss`, `validation` (leave-one-out
on the training pair), `simplicity`, `compression`, `mdl_program`
(2^{-form_length}), `flatness_proxy`, `weakness_oracle`,
`weakness_wrong_group`, `weakness_noisy_group`, `weakness_data_inferred`,
`random`.

Manifest:

- Command: `python -m experiments.symbolic_weakness.benchmark --trials-per-family 500 --seed 20260609 --out artifacts/symbolic_weakness/multi_family_500.json`
- Trials per family: 500
- Total selector evaluations: 22,000

## Gate

Acceptance:

- `weakness_oracle` must select the invariant family in at least 95% of
  trials on `cyclic_prefix_shift` and `dihedral_reflection`.
- `weakness_wrong_group` must select the invariant in less than 5% of
  trials on the same families (correct rejection).
- All other classical baselines (train_loss, simplicity, compression,
  mdl_program, flatness_proxy, validation) must select the invariant in
  less than 5% of trials on cyclic and dihedral.

Withheld:

- Do not claim weakness is universally superior. The benchmark exposes
  two structurally important negative cases (parity, color).
- Do not claim the result generalizes to natural-language or perception
  tasks without further experiments.

## Audit

Accepted:

- Cyclic and dihedral show a perfect separation: classical baselines
  pick the local shortcut on every trial; weakness picks the invariant
  on every trial. The 95% CIs do not overlap.
- The `weakness_wrong_group` control fails as expected, ruling out
  "any equivariance count works."
- `weakness_data_inferred` recovers the invariant in 100% of cyclic and
  dihedral trials without oracle group access.
- `weakness_noisy_group` is robust to a 50%+ corruption of the true
  group on cyclic and dihedral.

Rejected or withheld:

- Color permutation is a partial result. The S_n weakness count is not
  unique to the truth: wrong involutions can have comparable centralizer
  size. We do not claim weakness is universally optimal in S_n.
- Parity is a clean negative: |G|=2 is too small to separate truth
  from local patches that are also Z_2-equivariant.

Residual content:

- The benchmark reveals an operating regime for weakness as a selector:
  it requires a candidate group rich enough to separate the truth from
  train-perfect alternatives but small enough that wrong invariants do
  not also score high.
- The next question is whether learned group inference can extend
  weakness to families where the oracle group is not specified.

Next moves:

- Train neural networks on the same symbolic tasks and check whether
  representation-level weakness predicts OOD better than loss, sharpness,
  validation, or parameter norm.
- Build a learned-transformation pipeline that infers candidate groups
  from data, with wrong/noisy-group controls.
- Extend to compositional symmetries (Z_n × Z_m) and natural-language
  paraphrase invariants.
