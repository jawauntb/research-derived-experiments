## Discovery-Regime Audit

Question: in a controlled vocabulary where a short memorizer is available, does preferring the weakest compatible reusable rule generalize better than preferring the shortest form?

Current regime:

- Artifact types: Boolean worlds, candidate rule extensions, selector choices, Jaccard/accuracy metrics.
- Operations: generate target one-feature rule, sample observations, add memorizer candidate, choose by weakness/simplicity/random.
- Gates/verifiers: deterministic seed; weakness mean Jaccard should exceed simplicity by at least 0.15; unit tests should confirm selector behavior.
- Known limitations: target class is deliberately favorable to weakness; the memorizer is an artificial short-form trap.

Action class:

- Retrieval/search/discovery: search.
- Why: this explores behavior inside the current synthetic hypothesis schema.

Gate:

- Acceptance rule: weakness mean Jaccard - simplicity mean Jaccard >= 0.15.
- Withheld/rejected rule: if weakness overgeneralizes or ties simplicity, mark the toy as too weak and redesign.

Results:

- Accepted artifacts: this pilot summary.
- Rejected or withheld artifacts: raw JSON remains local-only in `artifacts/`.
- Key metrics: weakness Jaccard 0.9533, simplicity Jaccard 0.0938, margin 0.8596.
- Variance or ablation: not yet run.

Residual content:

- Explained by old regime: shortest-form memorization undergeneralizes in this synthetic setup.
- New content outside old regime: none yet.
- Retractions or supersessions: none.

Next move: add a negative control removing the memorizer and a stress test where the vocabulary includes overly broad unsafe hypotheses.
