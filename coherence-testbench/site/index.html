§0 Positioning

Objective brain-state biomarkers that make CNS and psychedelic trials work.

Sponsors, sites, and regulators need endpoints that hold up outside the clinic. We build them: passive, cross-subject readouts of attention, arousal, and altered state, delivered as measurement services trials can plug into.

§1 The problem

Modern CNS trials are still measured on self-report.

Depression, anxiety, PTSD, and psychedelic-adjunct studies lean almost entirely on subjective scales — PHQ-9, HAM-D, MADRS, CADSS. These instruments are noisy across raters, sensitive to expectancy, and increasingly compromised by functional unblinding: when patients can tell what they got, effect sizes distort in both directions.

The result is expensive Phase 2s that fail to replicate and Phase 3s that read out flat despite clear clinical signal. What's missing is not a better questionnaire. It's an objective measurement of what the brain is actually doing.

§2 Approach

An instrument, not a device.

We pair scalp EEG with functional near-infrared spectroscopy on commodity, research-grade hardware, then decode brain state cross-subject using models trained on a proprietary paired-data corpus. The output is a small set of continuous, session-level readouts a sponsor can drop into an existing CRF.

Signal

EEG + fNIRS, acquired on off-the-shelf hardware in-clinic or in-home. No custom silicon in the trial path.

Model

Cross-subject decoders — Riemannian alignment plus domain-adversarial heads — trained on a proprietary paired-data corpus. Leave-subjects-out is the only headline metric.

Delivery

Endpoint-as-a-service. Sponsors get calibrated readouts and analysis; we own the model, the reference data, and the regulatory dossier.

Discipline

Signal is separated from generative prior. Bandwidth reported as bits per second of mutual information, not accuracy alone. Corpus is license-clean.

§3 Regulatory ladder

From cleared instrument to qualified biomarker.

The path is deliberately conservative and sequential. Nothing about the platform requires a novel device classification; the moat is the reference corpus and the qualification package built on top of it.

Rung 1
510(k) — clearance as a general neurophysiological measurement system, predicate-based, on commodity acquisition hardware.
Rung 2
DDT / BQP — FDA biomarker qualification through the Drug Development Tool program, so the readout is usable across sponsors without re-validation in each IND.
Rung 3
Label-supportive endpoint — inclusion as a secondary or exploratory endpoint in registrational psychedelic and CNS programs.
