# Critical Review: When the World Responds

Date: 2026-07-06

## Verdict

Revise and strengthen. The paper is important because it finds both a positive
architecture result and a major epistemic failure: three-head decomposition
helps under action-correlated worlds, but learned probe selection goes silent
after a regime shift.

## Main Issues

1. **Name the re-engagement law.** The core failure is not just G7. It is that
   learned quiet can self-confirm after convergence. In changing worlds, quiet
   is not evidence of stability.
2. **Separate efficiency from adaptivity.** G5 shows the probe is selective and
   efficient during training. G7 shows that efficient selection is not enough
   for long-horizon agency.
3. **Make the oracle correction central.** Current attribution error is not
   value of information. Prior oracle baselines should be treated as weaker than
   their names suggested.
4. **Connect to agent architecture.** Real agents need an audit floor,
   surprise-triggered boost, or fast/slow detector to reopen inquiry after the
   world changes.
5. **Keep the claim bounded.** This is not a general nonstationary-world result;
   it is a controlled hazard setting that reveals the mechanism to test next.

## Contribution Opportunity

The major contribution is a diagnostic for long-horizon machine agency:

> Does the agent have a principled way to restart inquiry after its world model
> becomes quiet, or does it mistake learned silence for stability?

That question bridges self/world attribution, active probing, and robust
long-horizon behavior.
