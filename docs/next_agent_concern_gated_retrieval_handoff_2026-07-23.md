## Claim boundary

The only promoted claim is:

> On the role-authored graph family, dual-source diffusion plus a
> bounded-observer utilization filter discriminates the registered synthetic
> roles under frozen seeds and regimes.

Do not describe the current result as:

- learned memory geometry;
- recovery of concern from experience;
- proof that multiplicative fusion is necessary;
- human-memory or cognitive evidence;
- semantic meaning;
- selfhood or general intelligence; or
- deployment evidence.

The additive policy tied the product in base and sparse regimes. The initial,
learned, and oracle care conditions all achieved hit@1 `1.000`. Those are
preserved nulls, not inconveniences to optimize away.

## Core intuition to preserve

A bounded agent cannot keep everything active. It should retrieve facts where
two searches overlap:

1. what is relevant to the present context; and
2. what has persistent consequence for this agent.

The resulting candidates are only nominated. A separate bounded verifier asks
whether loading one actually improves the reachable future or external task
outcome.

The canonical example contrasts:

- a load-bearing date-sensitive commitment;
- related but useless calendar trivia; and
- a globally important but presently irrelevant alarm.

Concern is not equivalent to salience, urgency, reward, novelty, or semantic
similarity. Existing code and receipts sometimes call persistent concern
`care`; treat that as an implementation-era alias, not a separate construct.

## Next experiment program: COGR-E2

### Required target

A bounded retrieval policy evaluated by a sealed post-decision environment
outcome, first with fixed withheld geometry and then with graph structure
learned only from permitted episode history.

### Stage boundary

- **Wave 0:** premise audit plus calibration-only data; freeze effect sizes,
  sample sizes, features, hyperparameters, and analysis code. No calibration
  row may enter confirmation.
- **COGR-E2a:** cheap concern-recovery screen on fixed, withheld geometry, with
  randomized probes and logged propensities. This can reject the update rule
  but cannot establish learned geometry or L2.
- **COGR-E2b:** crossed learned/random/oracle geometry by
  frozen-wrong/learned/oracle concern. Use separate L1 and L2 gate receipts so
  concern-recovery failure cannot erase a valid retrieval result.

### Required design changes

1. Replace role-authored edges with fixed withheld geometry in E2a and learned
   graph structure in E2b.
2. Start with an adversarially misspecified concern prior that overweights a
   plausible alarm and underweights a true commitment.
3. Exercise both positive and negative care updates.
4. Seal role labels, answer keys, and utility from graph construction,
   retrieval, and care update.
5. Make initial, learned, oracle, additive, and product conditions
   non-ceiling.
6. Hold out full task templates, paraphrase families, and graph generators.
7. Score realized task outcomes as primary; use epiplexity only as a registered
   mediator or secondary diagnostic after an independent implementation or
   sensitivity check.
8. Force randomized exploration of initially suppressed regions, log selection
   propensities, and preregister off-policy or counterfactual estimation.
9. Audit permitted graph features for statistical leakage, not only direct
   evaluator-field access.
10. Type trusted and untrusted update sources, bound influence, preserve
    provenance, and test poisoning detection and rollback.

### Suggested procedural families

- delayed commitments;
- maintenance and fault response;
- resource-constrained planning.

All families should instantiate the same abstract retrieval problem through
different surface structure. A method that works only on the birthday-style
graph does not establish transfer.

### Minimum baselines

- no retrieval;
- random and frequency-only retrieval;
- modern embedding similarity;
- context-only and care-only diffusion;
- additive and multiplicative fusion;
- a learned one-stage ranker at matched capacity and compute;
- a learned one-stage ranker that consumes the same concern feature;
- information-matched value/advantage, task-priority, recency, eligibility, and
  learned-query second signals;
- larger context, hierarchical summaries, and recurrent compression at matched
  cost;
- wrong-agent and shuffled concern profiles;
- oracle graph and oracle care as ceilings only.

### Fatal gates

- zero evaluator-label or utility leakage;
- causal downstream contribution from learned geometry; held-out neighborhood
  quality remains diagnostic;
- sealed task-outcome gain over the best one-sided, semantic, additive,
  resource-allocation, and learned one-stage baselines;
- enough headroom to adjudicate additive/product and care-recovery contrasts;
- recovery from the wrong prior using exploratory and policy-selected positive
  and negative outcomes with valid propensity accounting;
- history-derived concern benefit beyond reward/value, task priority, salience,
  recency, semantics, and wrong-agent profiles;
- resistance or recovery from targeted concern poisoning;
- no preregistered task-family reversal hidden by the aggregate;
- reproducible manifests, seeds, row-level receipts, and rejected alternatives.

Additive equivalence blocks multiplicative-necessity claims only. A tie with
the best learned one-stage baseline blocks promotion of the two-stage
architecture. Care-recovery failure blocks L2+, not L1.

### Reuse boundary

Keep the existing graph/PPR numerical primitives, ranking and receipt schema,
manifest/provenance machinery, and epiplexity implementation for sensitivity
comparisons. Replace the generator, graph construction, concern update, and
future/outcome fixture. Add only learned-graph, sealed-outcome, propensity-log,
and concern-update interfaces with an immediate E2 consumer.

### Safety and data boundary

COGR-E2 evaluation is synthetic-only. The premise audit may use licensed public
or consented traces only after governance approval. Before any non-synthetic
trace, external memory, or public row-level release, approve a data-flow
inventory, lifecycle and deletion contract for raw and derived profiles,
actor/resource/operation matrix, identity-bound isolation and revocable
transfer rules, processor logging and training-use restrictions, and
disclosure/privacy review.

## Dependency order

1. **Audit the real-world premise under governance.**
   Estimate the prevalence and outcome cost of missed off-context constraints
   and whether history-derived concern explains failures beyond context and
   semantic retrieval. This gates broad usefulness claims, not the synthetic
   mechanism study.
2. **Build the anti-leakage and safety contracts.**
   Split policy observations from evaluator-only state; type trust boundaries
   and test that hidden or poisoned fields cannot silently redirect retrieval.
3. **Build procedural families and holdouts.**
   Generate histories before ranking; freeze family, paraphrase, and generator
   splits.
4. **Run calibration only.**
   Produce variance estimates and headroom checks, then freeze thresholds,
   sample sizes, features, hyperparameters, and analysis. Keep confirmatory
   templates inaccessible.
5. **Preregister E2a and E2b.**
   Record target object, representation/data clock, permitted graph-learning
   fields, sealed evaluator boundary, assumptions, fatal gates, controls,
   evidence paths, and promotion rules.
6. **Run E2a, then E2b.**
   Preserve every null and rejected alternative; report the crossed factorial
   contrasts rather than one aggregate winner.
7. **Audit each claim separately.**
   Promote only the lowest claim-ladder level whose necessary gates all pass.

## Advancement after COGR-E2

If L1 passes:

1. integrate it into one narrow live long-horizon agent beachhead with matched
   context, token, tool, latency, and retrieval budgets;
2. gate broad transfer on a preregistered task-success or
   constraint-preservation gain at acceptable cost;
3. only then transfer the frozen mechanism across symbolic,
   embedding-derived, temporal, and hybrid memory substrates;
4. stress attention hijacking, chronic-alarm monopoly, self-confirming care,
   privacy leakage, catastrophic forgetting, and memory-size scaling; and
5. seek an independent implementation that does not reuse the original graph
   generator or scorer.

The beachhead is a continuation gate, not L4 promotion. L3 still requires
cross-substrate transfer, and L4 requires stochastic/OOD live-agent validation.

If L1 fails, keep the failure and determine whether the shortcut was authored
geometry, a weak concern signal, or an inadequate fusion operator. If L2 fails,
retain the useful retrieval result but reject concern-recovery and self-model
language. Do not let L0 performance compensate for either failure.

## Research and publication strategy

The widely applicable contribution requires a stable interface:

- memory substrate;
- context seed;
- persistent concern state;
- bounded nomination budget;
- independently scored utilization or outcome.

Publishability depends first on beating the strongest simpler alternative, then
on showing that one frozen mechanism transfers across substrates and task
families—not on listing many hypothetical applications.
The strongest eventual package would include a theory paper, public benchmark,
row-level receipts, negative regimes, a live-agent study, and an independent
replication.
