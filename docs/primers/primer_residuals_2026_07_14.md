## Executive conclusion

The primer program is no longer a greenfield plan. It already has a structured evidence and claim kernel, five experiment manifests, one canonical gate-verdict migration, six corrected primers, an exact finite Bayesian value-of-information benchmark, a seed/bootstrap calibration study, a passive-active coupling and hysteresis study, a reusable synthetic causal-use API, a locked root quality environment, and the completed M5 reopen-trigger comparison.

M5 is complete as an experiment even though its universal superiority claim failed. F0, F1, F4, and F5 passed; F2 failed because the periodic trigger tied commitment latency, and F3 failed because a never-firing normalized trigger tied the zero false-reopen rate. The surviving claim is only that commitment change gave the best observed Pareto combination in this finite Suite C schedule. M5 is search inside an existing regime, not a discovery-regime transition or a neural continual-learning result.

The residual program is therefore narrower than the original six backlogs. It has four dominant bottlenecks:

- Finish the trust substrate: migrate structured gates, claims, manifests, status transitions, preregistration chronology, and provenance across the program.
- Make flagship evidence independently recomputable: public-safe rows, exact lineage, clean-clone verification, and outside replication bundles.
- Replace task-local positive stories with decisive mechanism tests: theorem assumptions, endogenous concern, graded causal use, dynamical return, viability, and many-part coordination.
- Add independence and program-level statistics: multiplicity, sequential stopping, adversarial control design, blinded claim-tier review, and external promotion criteria.

## How to read this register

- `complete` means the requested artifact or experiment exists. A strict FAIL or negative result can still complete an experiment.
- `stronger partial` means merged work materially narrows the residual but does not satisfy the original completion gate.
- `open` means the decisive deliverable has not been built or run.
- `invalid` means execution occurred but the frozen integrity gate blocked scientific interpretation; it must not be counted as a positive or negative verdict.
- IDs are preserved from the article-specific backlogs. Completed IDs are omitted from the residual lists unless needed to prevent accidental reruns.

This document optimizes overlap. The article sections preserve source ownership; the final execution waves combine duplicate work into one program sequence.

## Guardrails for closing residuals

- A negative or failed gate closes an experiment if the frozen test was executed correctly; it does not validate the positive hypothesis.
- An invalid integrity gate closes neither side of a scientific claim.
- A synthetic result may close a synthetic tranche while leaving empirical transport open.
- A reading task closes only when its sources change a claim boundary, control, estimator, schema, or decision rule.
- No item closes from prose alone when its gate asks for executable evidence.
- Do not reopen a completed negative by tuning thresholds or controls against its outcomes. A replacement requires a new question and preregistration.
- Treat current manifests, registries, and root CI as a foundation to migrate and extend, not as absent infrastructure and not as universal coverage.
