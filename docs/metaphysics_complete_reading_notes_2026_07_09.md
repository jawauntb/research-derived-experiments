### Theory / framing
Operationalizes Bennett weakness as cardinality of group elements under which a hypothesis remains with-action equivariant. Conjecture: when train-perfect shortcuts and invariant rules coexist, weakness under the true group selects the OOD-generalizing rule where loss, MDL, simplicity, flatness, and validation fail.

### Methods
Four symbolic families (cyclic, dihedral, parity, \(S_n\)), 500 trials each, 11 selectors. Neural: 256 local + 1024 Modal MLPs on cyclic prefix-shift; also rotated-stroke vision (96 models) and Pythia-70M paraphrase probe. Controls: wrong-group, noisy-group, data-inferred group, random labels.

### Key findings (PASS/FAIL with numbers)

- **PASS** neural weakness↔OOD: Pearson \(r=+0.817\) (256), \(+0.813\) (1024); loss/val/sharpness \(|r|\le 0.14\).
- **PARTIAL PASS** color_permutation: weakness_oracle 0.824; data-inferred only 0.138.
- **FAIL** parity_coset: weakness_oracle 0.000 (group too small).
- **PASS** vision strokes: \(r=+0.672\) vs wrong-group \(r=-0.341\).
- **PARTIAL** language: centered paraphrase gap up to +0.793 (layer 5) but Pearson(weakness, next-token behavior) in \([-0.35,-0.13]\) — geometry yes, behavior chain no.

### Rejected alternatives
Train-loss, validation, simplicity, compression, MDL, flatness_proxy, wrong-group weakness, “any equivariance count works.”

### Next directions / open questions
Scale beyond toys; neural group discovery; weakness as training regularizer; PAC-Bayes link; larger LM paraphrase with log-prob metrics; compositional groups.

### Claim boundary
