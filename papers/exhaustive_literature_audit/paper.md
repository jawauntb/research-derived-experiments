# Exhaustive Literature and PDF Audit for the Research-Derived Experiments Corpus

Date: 2026-07-07

## Abstract

This paper completes the assignment that earlier review drafts did not fully satisfy: enumerate and audit every citation item cited in the repository's paper corpus, and review every tracked paper PDF stored in the repository. The audit covers 110 tracked paper-source files under `papers/`, including 45 primary papers, 30 preregistrations or runbooks, 24 supporting paper sources, 9 README files, and 2 BibTeX files. It extracts 177 unique citation/reference items from those papers and reviews 60 tracked PDFs by extracting text from every page. The paper distinguishes four evidence depths: full local PDF review for tracked PDFs; internal companion-source enumeration for repository citations; bibliographic/context review for external citations not stored locally as PDFs; and unresolved raw reference fragments where a paper cited a literature cluster or note rather than one clean bibliographic item. The resulting synthesis is stricter than the earlier literature review: it treats reference hygiene, missing source provenance, and overclaim boundaries as first-class findings rather than footnotes.

## 1. Correction to the prior deliverable

The prior paper, `papers/comprehensive_literature_review/paper.md`, was a strong internal synthesis but it was not an exhaustive literature audit. It did not fully enumerate every citation item, did not review every tracked PDF, and did not mark which cited works had full local text available. This paper corrects that.

The correction is methodological, not cosmetic. A literature review is only as good as its source discipline. If a paper cites "habituation literature" or "calibrated active learning literature" without an atomic bibliographic item, the review must not pretend that a clean source was reviewed. If a citation is external and no PDF is stored locally, the review can use available bibliographic context, but it must not claim full-text review. If a PDF is tracked in the repo, the review can and should extract all of it.

## 2. Audit method

The audit was generated by `scripts/build_exhaustive_literature_audit.py`.

The script performs five operations:

1. Enumerates tracked paper-source files using `git ls-files papers/**/*.md`, `papers/**/*.tex`, and `papers/**/*.bib`.
2. Parses titles and classifies files as primary papers, preregistrations/runbooks, supporting paper sources, READMEs, or bibliographies.
3. Extracts reference sections from Markdown and TeX papers and BibTeX entries from paper packages.
4. Deduplicates references into a citation ledger with kind, review status, topic tags, source files, and audit notes.
5. Enumerates tracked PDFs under `papers/**/*.pdf` and `output/**/*.pdf`, then extracts text from every page with `pypdf`.

The generated ledgers are part of the paper artifact:

- `papers/exhaustive_literature_audit/audit_summary.json`
- `papers/exhaustive_literature_audit/paper_source_ledger.csv`
- `papers/exhaustive_literature_audit/paper_source_ledger.json`
- `papers/exhaustive_literature_audit/citation_ledger.csv`
- `papers/exhaustive_literature_audit/citation_ledger.json`
- `papers/exhaustive_literature_audit/pdf_review_ledger.csv`
- `papers/exhaustive_literature_audit/pdf_review_ledger.json`

The audit found:

- 110 tracked paper-source files.
- 45 primary papers.
- 30 preregistrations or runbooks.
- 24 supporting paper-source files.
- 9 paper READMEs.
- 2 BibTeX files.
- 177 unique citation/reference items.

The citation ledger contains:

- 17 external linked citations.
- 26 external bibliographic citations.
- 39 internal repository/program citations.
- 14 non-atomic literature cluster notes.
- 81 ambiguous raw reference fragments preserved for cleanup.

The ambiguous category is high because many papers cite literature clusters in prose, such as "habituation literature," "BALD / value of information," "comparator model literature," or "same six-cluster citation stack as P21A." This is an important audit finding. The research program has strong source coverage, but its reference sections should be normalized if the papers are prepared for external publication.

## 4. STORM-style multi-perspective audit

### Practitioner perspective

The practical result is useful: the repository now has a source ledger, citation ledger, and PDF review ledger that can be regenerated. The strongest improvement is operational traceability. A future paper editor can now ask, "Where did this claim come from?" and find the source file, citation item, or PDF row.

What this perspective notices: the audit is not just scholarship; it is build infrastructure for scientific hygiene.

### Academic perspective

The academic perspective is stricter. The corpus cites many real research areas, but many entries are not publication-grade references yet. External citation rows without local PDFs should not be described as full literature review. Citation clusters need atomic references. Local PDFs were reviewed by extraction, but extraction is not the same as human close reading of every figure, table, and proof.

What this perspective notices: the paper can claim exhaustive enumeration and local-PDF text review, but not universal full-text review of every external work.

### Skeptic perspective

The skeptic asks whether the repository is laundering its own papers into external evidence. Many citations are internal program papers. That is fine for lineage, but not for independent validation. The skeptic also notices that "unclear reference" rows are not a small nuisance; they are 81 of 177 citation items.

What this perspective notices: the strongest v3 contribution is not a grand synthesis. It is the exposure of reference debt.

### Incentives perspective

The incentives perspective says the program benefits from being honest before reviewers force honesty. It is tempting to market a comprehensive literature review as if every cited paper were fully read and externally verified. The better incentive is durable credibility: mark review depth, missing PDFs, unresolved clusters, and local-only evidence.

What this perspective notices: explicit provenance is a reputational asset.

### Historian perspective

Historically, research programs become stronger when they build bibliographic discipline after the first burst of exploratory work. This repository has moved fast, generated many papers, and recorded many negative results. The next maturation step is normalizing citations, source manifests, benchmark cards, and evidence matrices.

What this perspective notices: the audit marks a transition from discovery sprint to publication infrastructure.

## 5. Contradiction map

There is a productive contradiction between the practical and academic views.

The practical view says the audit is enough to move forward: every paper source, citation item, and tracked PDF is now enumerated. The academic view says enumeration is not the same as close reading of every external citation. The skeptic agrees with the academic view but adds a sharper worry: internal companion papers can make the program look more externally grounded than it is.

The strongest evidence is on the academic/skeptic side for claim boundaries. This paper should therefore claim:

> Exhaustive local-corpus audit, citation enumeration, and tracked-PDF text review.

It should not claim:

> Full-text review of every external cited work, including sources not stored in the repository.

The next resolving action is clear: fetch or attach every external cited paper where licensing permits, normalize every cluster note into atomic references, and rerun the audit with `review_depth = external_full_text_reviewed` only where that is true.

## 6. What the citations collectively say

The citation surface falls into seven clusters.

### 6.1 Causality and identifiability

The causal cluster includes Pearl-style mediation, weakly supervised causal representation learning, causal world models, interventional agents, Causal-JEPA, CausaLab, and object-centric causal representation learning. The invariant is that passive observation underdetermines latent cause. This directly supports the repository's reafferent-identifiability line: self/world attribution, mediated effects, and deployment-shift rules need intervention or constraint.

### 6.2 Representation learning and probing

The representation cluster includes disentanglement limits, probe-control warnings, concept/persona vectors, activation editing, and cross-model representational transfer. The invariant is that internal geometry can be useful but is not automatically causal. The repository's stricter standard is intervention-specificity: a representation matters when changing it changes behavior in the intended way and controls fail.

### 6.3 Active learning and uncertainty

The uncertainty cluster includes BALD, calibrated active learning, epistemic neural networks, dropout uncertainty, deep ensembles, curiosity, random network distillation, CUSUM/Page-Hinkley, and active inference precision tuning. The repository's contribution is a corrective: uncertainty is not automatically inquiry value, and inquiry value is not automatically adaptive re-engagement.

### 6.4 Domain generalization, symmetry, and structure

The OOD cluster includes underspecification, invariant risk minimization, DomainBed, GroupDRO, WILDS, shortcut learning, group equivariance, neural symmetry learning, grokking, flat/sharp minima, MDL, and geometric deep learning. The repository's strongest external-facing synthesis is here: when training evidence underdetermines the rule, deployment-compatible transformations can select better models than ordinary fit proxies.

### 6.5 Agent benchmarks and tool use

The agent cluster includes ReAct, Reflexion, Voyager, SayCan, Tree of Thoughts, Toolformer, AgentBench, WebArena, OSWorld, GAIA, SWE-bench, BIG-bench, HELM, concrete AI safety problems, and goal misgeneralization. The repository adds a proxy-resistant benchmark stance: behavior scores should be paired with causal-structure gates and anti-cheat controls.

### 6.6 Biology, mind, and cybernetics

The biology/mind cluster includes Ashby, autopoiesis, enactivism, TAME, free energy/active inference, affordances, Canguilhem, Jonas, Simondon, Uexkull, Vervaeke, Bennett, and virtual-governor framing. The repository should use this literature as conceptual framing for concern, viability, boundary maintenance, and relevance, while withholding consciousness claims.

### 6.7 Geometry and mathematical structure

The geometry cluster includes geometric deep learning, group representation, spectral learning for group composition, grid-cell/topological geometry, rate-distortion, efficient coding, and reward-deformed metrics. The key invariant is that finite compression under structure creates geometry. The repository's strongest mathematical frontier is to connect weakness, spectra, topology, and concern-weighted capacity allocation.

## 8. Revised research synthesis after exhaustive audit

After reviewing the citation surface and all tracked PDFs, the core research program can be stated more precisely:

> A finite agent is causally grounded to the extent that variables relevant to future viability, attribution, inquiry, memory, and deployment shift are represented at the action surfaces where behavior is selected, and to the extent that proxy explanations fail under anti-cheat controls.

The strongest supported internal laws remain:

1. Predictive policy closure: action-conditioned concern predictions can become a policy when action selection closes through them.
2. Reafferent identifiability: self/world attribution requires a gauge-breaking signal.
3. Re-engagement floor: quiet after convergence is not evidence of stability unless inquiry can reopen after surprise.
4. Commitment-surface memory: memory matters when it reaches the future action interface.
5. Structure-compatible selection: under underspecification, deployment transformation compatibility can beat ordinary fit proxies.
6. Stress transduction: global constraint violation must be represented where local action is selected.

The strongest withheld claims are equally important:

1. The corpus does not prove consciousness.
2. The corpus does not prove universal OOD robustness.
3. The corpus does not prove that every external citation has been fully reviewed.
4. The corpus does not prove that local generated PDFs are independent external evidence.
5. The corpus does not yet normalize all literature-cluster references into publication-grade bibliography entries.

## 10. Publication cleanup required

The audit finds that the next publication pass should:

1. Convert ambiguous reference fragments into atomic citations.
2. Split internal companion papers from external references in every bibliography.
3. Add DOI, arXiv, venue, and year fields where missing.
4. Attach or fetch full texts for external citations where allowed.
5. Mark review depth per citation: bibliographic, abstract, full text, local PDF, or primary result artifact.
6. Avoid citing broad clusters such as "habituation literature" without representative sources.
7. Keep virtual-governor and consciousness-adjacent sources as framing unless directly tested.
8. Treat generated local papers as internal evidence, not independent confirmation.

## 11. Conclusion

This paper finally performs the assignment at the level the repository can currently support. Every tracked paper source was inventoried. Every citation/reference item extracted from the paper corpus was preserved in a ledger. Every tracked PDF under the paper/output corpus was text-extracted across all pages and summarized. The resulting literature picture is stronger and humbler than the earlier review.

The program has a coherent scientific spine: finite agents become more trustworthy when viability-relevant variables become causal at action surfaces and proxy explanations are eliminated. But the audit also shows bibliographic debt. The next v4 should not be another broad synthesis. It should be a reference-normalization and external-full-text pass that turns every ambiguous citation cluster into a clean bibliographic row with explicit review depth.
