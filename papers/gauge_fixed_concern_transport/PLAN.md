# Gauge-Fixed Concern Transport Paper Plan

## Objective

Write a self-contained, proof-first paper that does not rely on the reader knowing the prior research corpus. The paper should introduce concern as a measure over relevant distinctions, prove that concern-weighted weakness generalizes finite weakness, and then show that a distinction becomes scientifically meaningful only when it is transported, gauge-fixed, and load-bearing at a commitment surface.

## Core Thesis

A finite agent does not merely possess representations in space. It transports concern-weighted distinctions through transformations, abstractions, time, interventions, and memory until those distinctions reach a surface where future control is committed.

The central bridge bound is:

```text
Load_gamma(h) >= (initial concern mass - transport loss)
                * (gauge-fixed commitment effect).
```

## Theorem Ladder

1. Recover Bennett-style finite weakness as the uniform concern case.
2. Generalize weakness to nonuniform concern-weighted extension mass.
3. Recover group-block OOD generalization as a special case of concern-weighted weakness.
4. Prove a telescoping transport-loss bound.
5. Prove gauge non-identifiability without interventions or commitments.
6. Define commitment effect and load-bearing concern.
7. Prove the gauge-fixed transport bridge theorem.
8. Derive corollaries for equivariance, mechanistic interpretability, reafference, allostasis, type theory, and sheaf gluing.

## What Makes It Useful

The paper turns broad philosophical claims into experimental proof obligations:

- What distinction matters?
- How is it weighted by concern?
- Through which contexts must it be transported?
- Which gauge-equivalent alternatives remain?
- What intervention fixes the gauge?
- Where is the commitment surface?
- Does the distinction change the commitment?

## Cross-Field Demonstrations

- ML robustness: concern-weighted model selection under deployment shifts.
- Causal representation learning: interventions as gauge fixing.
- Mechanistic interpretability: probes versus causal patching or editing.
- Neuroscience: reafference and corollary discharge as self/world gauge fixing.
- Allostasis: time-varying concern density over future bodily demand.
- Philosophy of mind: intentionality and meaning as transported concern-to-commitment.
- Mathematics: group theory for transformations, category theory for transport, type theory for evidence obligations, topology/sheaves for gluing.

## Empirical Next Steps

4. Long-horizon tool-agent task where the operative memory bottleneck is moved and patched.
